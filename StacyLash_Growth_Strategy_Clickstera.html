The next 30 days · Google

A four-week plan that builds proof before it spends big.

Weeks 1–2 are search-only, sending traffic straight to Amazon — clean baseline data first. Weeks 3–4 open new channels to learn where sales come cheapest.

Set up tracking, launch one campaign, watch for 7–8 days

Search only · traffic direct to Amazon
  • Set up Amazon Attribution on the Google campaigns so we can see real Amazon clicks and purchases.
  • Launch one Search campaign with three ad groups: Lash Shampoo · all Lash Glues · all other products — each loaded with the proven keywords.
  • Send every click straight to the Amazon listing. No shopping ads, no landing page yet.
  • Let it run 7–8 days to find the overall winners.
Why direct-to-Amazon first: it gives us a clean baseline of what Amazon traffic is worth before we add any extra steps.

Split into two focused campaigns for tighter control

Campaign-level optimization on the proven winners
  • Break the best performers into two dedicated campaigns: one for Lash Shampoo, one for Lash Glue.
  • Each gets its own budget and the keywords that already performed in Week 1.
  • Keep the original campaign running in parallel to keep surfacing new winners.
Still search-only and still direct to Amazon through Week 2 — we don't add variables until the base data is solid.

Add shopping and begin the landing-page test

Now we learn where sales come cheapest
  • Launch Shopping campaigns to the Shopify website to grow website sales and read those numbers.
  • Begin testing a landing page that routes buyers to Amazon, with a conversion action fired on Google Ads so the campaigns can learn and optimize for the future.
  • Start preparing Lash Serum for both platforms (see next section).

By month-end, three clean data streams to compare

This becomes the roadmap for months 2 & 3
  • Website Shopping campaign performance.
  • Landing-page route (Google → page → Amazon) with real conversion learning.
  • Direct Amazon traffic from Search campaigns.
Comparing the three tells us exactly where to put budget next — and which setup teaches Google's bidding the most.
